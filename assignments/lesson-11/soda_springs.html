    Color Sky 5K

    The Fourth of July is over, fireworks have been put away, and everyone is moving on with their lives. Everyone except for the brave men and women who are left battling the Grizzly Bear fire. This 500 acre fire was started on July 3rd by nature’s own fireworks when a lightning storm passed through the south side of the valley. There was an estimated 900 lightning strikes within an hour time span.
    “We’re really lucky that with such a big lightning storm, there weren’t more fires started,” said Chief Clark of the Soda Springs Fire Department. “Things are just really dry and all it takes is a little spark.”
    It will take another few days for the fire to be 100% contained. Until then expect to see smoky skies as the wind picks up and blows the smoke our direction. Just as Chief Brown warned, conditions are hot and dry. Please be vigilant when using backyard firepits, or camping in the area. Make sure all fires are completely drenched with water. A little effort can keep us all safe.
